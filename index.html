Dr. Muhammad Yunus’s “Three Zeros” theory focuses on achieving zero poverty, zero unemployment, and zero net carbon emissions. This theory aims to create a more equitable and sustainable world by addressing economic, social, and environmental challenges simultaneously. The theory emphasizes the importance of social business and good governance to achieve these goals. Social businesses are enterprises designed to solve social problems and serve humanity, rather than maximizing profit for shareholders. They operate on the principle of reinvesting profits into the business to further its social mission. To implement the “Three Zeros” theory effectively, the following strategies are recommended: Encouraging youth engagement and entrepreneurship Leveraging technology to enhance efficiency and reach Promoting sustainable economic practices Fostering good governance to ensure fair policies and regulations The government of Bangladesh is considering incorporating the “Three Zeros” theory into its Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) strategy, recognizing its potential to accelerate progress towards these global targets.
